FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the historical significance of Marinduque National High School?
Marinduque National High School (MNHS) is historically significant because it pioneered public secondary education in the province of Marinduque. Established in 1913 during the American regime, it provided local students access to high school education without needing to travel to Manila or Lucena, Quezon. For over a century, MNHS has shaped generations of educated, responsible, and civic-minded citizens in the province.
How did MNHS begin and who established it?
MNHS was first established in 1913 as Boac High School under the American government’s program to mass educate Filipinos. The American Thomasites were sent to organize and manage the school. It initially opened with around 20 first-year students. In 1914, it was renamed Marinduque High School.
How did enrollment change during the early years of the school?
In 1914, there were 23 enrolled students, and two of them were recognized as honor students. Enrollment dropped to 14 students in 1916 and increased again to 22 in 1918. Over time, the number of students gradually increased, and by 1923, a complete high school program was already offered.
How did World War II affect MNHS?
During World War II, especially in 1944–1945, many schools in the Philippines faced destruction and disruption. Despite these challenges, MNHS survived and continued to operate after the war. The school endured difficulties and rebuilding efforts, proving its resilience and commitment to education.
What caused the temporary decrease in enrollment in 1951?
Enrollment gradually decreased in 1951 due to the establishment of different private schools in the province. However, MNHS continued to grow and remained a strong educational institution in Marinduque.
What major developments happened in the 1950s under school leadership?
Full-scale development began in 1956 under the first homegrown principal, Mrs. Elena Nieva Jambalos. During her leadership, important buildings such as the Home Economics Building, Lecaroz Building, and Dela Santa Building were constructed. These developments marked the expansion and modernization of the school facilities.
When and how did the school become Marinduque National High School?
In 1982, a bill was sponsored proposing the nationalization of Marinduque High School. It was signed into law on November 13, 1982. The law officially took effect in August 1984 when funding for its operation was released. This officially converted the school into Marinduque National High School.
What important infrastructure developments happened in the late 1980s and 1990s?
In 1989, the proposed budget for the addition of three buildings was approved. The old Dela Santa Building was demolished and replaced with a two-storey building. From 1993 to 1995, the Tower Watch was constructed. These developments helped accommodate the growing student population and modernize the campus.
How did technology become part of MNHS history?
In 1997, the school officially opened its computer room with only one functioning computer. Through donations from supporters and institutions, the number of computers increased to 21 functioning units housed in an air-conditioned room at the JICA Building. This marked the beginning of technological advancement in the school.
What academic improvements were introduced in the late 1990s?
During the school year 1997–1998, MNHS introduced the Special Science Class Curriculum. This program strengthened the academic offerings of the school and provided enhanced learning opportunities for students interested in science.
Why is MNHS recognized as a historic institution today?
MNHS is recognized as a historic institution because of its century-long contribution to education in Marinduque. It has survived wars, enrollment challenges, and infrastructure changes while continuing to grow and improve. Its long history and impact on the province have made it a beacon of education and leadership for generations.
