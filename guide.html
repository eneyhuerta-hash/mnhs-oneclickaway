STUDENT'S GUIDE

Punctuality & Attendance

Arrive at school at the designated time. Attend the Monday morning Flag Raising Ceremony.

Uniform & Identification

Enter the school premises wearing the proper uniform and I.D.

Grooming

Maintain a neat haircut and natural hair color. Fancy hairstyles, excessive hair products (stiff hair), or dyed hair are not permitted.

Class Attendance

Attend all subjects daily. In case of absence, parents must provide a written excuse letter to the adviser stating the reason.

Academic Effort

Complete and submit projects for every subject on time to ensure prompt grading. Study diligently to pass every subject.

Conduct

Respect all teachers, fellow students, and school staff or authorities.

MINOR OFFENSES
  • Cutting classes.
  • Using another student’s I.D. or lending your I.D. to others.
  • Jumping over the fence or leaving school grounds outside of designated hours.
  • Cheating or providing answers to classmates during examinations.
  • Wearing excessive makeup or having tattoos/markings on any part of the body.
  • Wearing accessories not suited for the school uniform (e.g., hats, earrings for males, heavy chains, etc.).
  • Bringing or entertaining outside guests inside the school (except parents).
  • Littering inside or outside the school; loitering or making noise in hallways while classes are in session.
  • Selling items inside the classroom unless it is part of a school subject.
MAJOR OFFENSES
  • Public Display of Affection (PDA).
  • Taking or damaging property that does not belong to you.
  • Vandalizing school property (tables, chairs, walls, etc.).
  • Writing on walls, tables, chairs, or any part of the school premises.
  • Smoking, bringing alcoholic beverages, or entering the campus/class under the influence of alcohol.
  • Forging or falsifying signatures and tampering with any official school records.
  • Shouting at or harming fellow students/staff, and blatant defiance of authority.
  • Profanity and use of obscene or defamatory language.
  • Extortion or forcibly asking for money or items.
  • Unauthorized use of cellphones, laptops, and other gadgets during class hours.
  • Watching obscene/pornographic materials.
  • Taking photos or recording conversations/actions of students or staff without consent.
  • Uploading or spreading videos/pictures that damage the reputation of teachers, students, or staff.
  • Forming or joining unauthorized organizations (Fraternities/Sororities/Gangs) per DepEd Order No. 20, s. 1991.
GRAVE OFFENSES
  • Bringing or smuggling any deadly weapons.
  • Use, possession, or sale of prohibited drugs.
  • Hazing inside or outside the school.
  • Bullying that results in physical injury or psychological disturbance.
METHODS

a. Agreement/Contract
b. Suspension
c. Transfer
d. Dismissal/Expulsion
e. Referral

STAGES

FIRST OFFENSE: Student is called for a meeting with the Class Adviser for a warning.

SECOND OFFENSE: Parents are summoned to discuss the violation and apply the necessary discipline and written agreement.

THIRD OFFENSE: Parents are summoned to be informed of the final decision (sanction) by the School Principal.

MANDATES & LAWS

Disciplinary action for violations of Philippine Laws will follow due process based on:

  • R.A. 11053: Anti-Hazing Law
  • R.A. 10627: Anti-Bullying Act of 2013
  • R.A. 10175: Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2000
  • R.A. 11313: Safe Spaces Act (Bawal Bastos Law)
  • R.A. 9165: Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002
  • R.A. 9514: Fire Code of the Philippines of 2008
  • R.A. 8353: Anti-Rape Law of 1997
  • R.A. 7610: Special Protection of Children Act
  • R.A. 9344: Juvenile Justice and Welfare System Act
